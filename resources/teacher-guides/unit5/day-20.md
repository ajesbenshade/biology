# Teacher Guide: Unit 5 Day 20 – Unit Review & Error Analysis

> **For teacher use only.** This guide contains review structure, sample misconceptions, and grading supports.

## Scripture Focus

**Proverbs 2:6 + 1 Thessalonians 5:21**

Tie in:
> “Review is an act of testing and refining. We pursue truth with diligence, hold fast to what is sound, and correct what is weak.”

## Teacher Objectives

- Review key Unit 5 concepts.
- Correct common errors in evidence interpretation and mechanism application.
- Prepare students for the summative assessment.
- Strengthen confidence through retrieval and revision.

## Background for the Teacher

This day should revisit:
- evidence lines for evolutionary claims
- microevolutionary mechanisms
- Hardy-Weinberg and population data
- speciation terms
- extinction, conservation, and sustainability
- evidence vs. inference distinction throughout the unit

The emphasis is not only memorization but **precision**.

## Key Biblical Integration Points

- “Accurate recall and correction build wisdom.”
- “Science serves best when subordinated to God’s truth.”
- “Testing everything includes testing our own explanations for overreach or vagueness.”

## Materials & Preparation

- Review station prompts.
- Anonymized sample errors.
- Short readiness-check quiz.
- Study guide or review packet.

## Bell Work (8 min)

**Prompt:** Give one quick contrast, such as allopatric vs. sympatric speciation.

**Model answer:**
> “Allopatric speciation involves geographic separation; sympatric speciation happens without geographic separation but still involves reproductive isolation.”

**Scoring:** 5 pts for effort and basic accuracy.

## Lesson Delivery (70 min)

### 1. Stations Rotation (35 min)

Suggested stations:
- evidence lines and interpretation
- natural selection / drift / gene flow / sexual selection
- Hardy-Weinberg and population data
- speciation, extinction, conservation

Students answer, compare, and check.

### 2. Error Analysis Workshop (20 min)

Use anonymized sample errors.

Examples:
- “Microevolution automatically proves every macroevolutionary claim.”
- “Hardy-Weinberg deviation always means natural selection.”
- “Speciation always requires physical distance.”
- “Conservation is unbiblical environmentalism.”

Have students rewrite each more accurately.

### 3. Readiness Check (10 min)

Use a mixed mini-quiz with vocabulary, short explanation, and one evidence/inference item.

### 4. Final Reflection (5 min)

Prompt:
> “What is one area of growth for you before the assessment?”

## Homework Grading

- **Review packet + corrections** — 10 pts
- **Study guide completion** — 5 pts

## Common Confusions and Fixes

- **“Observable micro-change always proves macro-history.”**  
  Fix: “Observable micro-change is real evidence, but broader historical reconstruction involves additional interpretation.”
- **“If I know vocabulary, I’m fully ready.”**  
  Fix: “Readiness also requires explanation, application, and distinguishing evidence from inference.”
- **“Error correction means failure.”**  
  Fix: “No. Error correction is one of the best paths to mastery.”

## Differentiation

- **Struggling learners:** provide station answer frames and a shortened checklist.
- **Advanced learners:** add one synthesis prompt connecting multiple unit topics.
- **ELL support:** vocabulary review cards with examples and non-examples.

## Prayer Close

> “Lord, grant wisdom for the assessment. Help us remember clearly, correct humbly, and hold fast to what is true.”
