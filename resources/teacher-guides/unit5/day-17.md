# Teacher Guide: Unit 5 Day 17 – 16.2 Extinction and Biodiversity Loss

> **For teacher use only.** This guide contains background notes, discussion supports, and grading guidance.

## Scripture Focus

**Genesis 2:15 + Romans 8:19–22**

> “The Lord God took the man and put him in the Garden of Eden to work it and take care of it.”

Tie in:
> “Extinction is real and tragic. It reflects a fallen world under decay, yet humans are still called to steward what God has entrusted to us.”

## Teacher Objectives

- Define background extinction and mass extinction.
- Explain major causes of modern biodiversity loss.
- Interpret extinction-rate graphs and hotspot maps.
- Distinguish natural extinction from accelerated anthropogenic loss.

## Background for the Teacher

**Background extinction** is the normal, relatively low rate at which species disappear over time.

**Mass extinction** is a rapid, large-scale loss of many species across broad regions and ecosystems.

Modern biodiversity studies often estimate current extinction rates at roughly **100–1,000 times** the background rate, though exact estimates vary by method.

**Biodiversity hotspots** are regions with:
- high endemism
- high species richness
- high threat from habitat destruction or other pressures

Examples often include Madagascar, the Tropical Andes, and parts of Southeast Asia.

## Key Biblical Integration Points

- “Extinction reminds us creation groans under the curse.”
- “Conservation is dominion exercised humbly and compassionately.”
- “We care for creation because it belongs to God, not because nature is ultimate.”

## Materials & Preparation

- Printed extinction-rate graphs.
- Biodiversity hotspot map.
- Cause cards for sorting: habitat loss, overexploitation, invasive species, pollution, climate change.
- Bibles or printed Scripture excerpt.

## Bell Work (8 min)

**Prompt:** What is one reason species go extinct today?

**Model answers:**
- Basic: “People destroy their homes.”
- Good: “Habitat loss from farming or cities.”
- Strong: “Human actions can accelerate extinction rates, which highlights our stewardship responsibility.”

**Scoring:** 5 pts for effort and relevance.

## Lesson Delivery (70 min)

### 1. Scripture + Intro (5 min)

Read Genesis 2:15 and Romans 8:19–22.

Say:
> “God calls people to care for creation. Today we examine what threatens biodiversity and how Christians should think about it.”

### 2. Extinction Types and Causes (15 min)

Teach:
- background extinction = slow, ordinary loss over time
- mass extinction = rapid, large-scale loss

Introduce five major drivers:
- habitat loss
- overexploitation
- invasive species
- pollution
- climate-related change

Say:
> “Many modern losses are linked to human action. That means our choices matter.”

### 3. Graph and Map Interpretation (20 min)

Students annotate the extinction-rate graph and hotspot map.

Key interpretation points:
- spike on the graph = acceleration in loss rate
- hotspots combine biological richness with high vulnerability

Ask:
- Where are hotspots clustered?
- What pressures threaten these regions?
- Why does a hotspot deserve focused attention?

### 4. Cause Sorting and Discussion (20 min)

Groups sort cause cards by likely impact and justify their reasoning.

Teacher line:
> “Christians respond to biodiversity loss with stewardship, not panic and not indifference.”

### 5. Quick Reflection (10 min)

Prompt:
> “Name one practical action humans can take to reduce biodiversity loss.”

Examples:
- protect habitat
- reduce overharvesting
- control invasive species
- reduce pollution

## Exit Ticket

**Prompt:** Name one driver of biodiversity loss and one biblical principle that should shape our response.

**Key:**
- driver: habitat loss, pollution, overexploitation, invasive species, climate-related change
- principle: stewardship / dominion under God / care for creation

**Scoring:** 5 pts

## Homework Grading

- **Graph and map questions** — 10 pts
- **Paragraph: human role in extinction** — 5 pts

**Rubric:**
- Data accuracy: 50%
- Biblical tie: 30%
- Clarity: 20%

## Common Confusions and Fixes

- **“All extinction is equally bad.”**  
  Fix: “Some background extinction is part of normal ecological history. Accelerated human-caused loss is different.”
- **“Conservation means worshiping nature.”**  
  Fix: “No. Conservation is stewardship under God.”
- **“Humans cannot make any meaningful difference.”**  
  Fix: “Evidence shows human choices can either increase harm or support recovery.”

## Differentiation

- **Struggling learners:** provide labeled maps and sentence frames.
- **Advanced learners:** assign short research on one biodiversity hotspot.
- **ELL support:** pre-teach extinction, hotspot, endemic, habitat, invasive.

## Prayer Close

> “Lord, help us care for what You have entrusted to us and respond to creation’s groaning with wisdom and obedience.”
