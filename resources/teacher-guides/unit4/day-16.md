# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 16 – Practice Assessment and Readiness Conferences

> **For teacher use only.** This guide contains readiness criteria and conference prompts not intended for student view.

---

## Scripture Focus

**Isaiah 41:10**
> "So do not fear, for I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God."

Many students feel pressure on the day before a unit assessment. This verse helps frame the block with steadiness rather than panic. The goal is calm clarity about what is strong and what still needs one last review.

---

## Teacher Objectives

- Use a short practice assessment to estimate Day 17 readiness.
- Give every student one concrete strength and one concrete next step.
- Preserve a calm, diagnostic tone rather than a high-pressure atmosphere.
- Use the longer class block to make conferences useful instead of rushed.

**Background for you:** This day works best when it feels calm and narrow. Students do not need a flood of last-minute advice. They need one accurate picture of readiness, one honest strength, and one manageable target for the final evening of review.

---

## Key Biblical Integration Points

- "Preparation is not the same thing as fear. Students can work seriously without spiraling into panic."
- "Truthful self-assessment is part of wisdom. Students should leave knowing both a real strength and a real next step."
- "Encouragement should be honest. We steady students best when we name what is strong and what still needs attention."

---

## Materials & Preparation

- Short mixed-format practice assessment.
- Conference tracker with a line for one strength and one next step per student.
- Any accommodations that will also apply on Day 17.
- A short review-reference sheet students can use after conferences.

---

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Have assessment copies, seating, and accommodations ready before students arrive.
- Keep the launch calm and procedural; do not spend the first minutes adding pressure.
- Pull conferences in a predictable order so students know they will be seen.
- End each conference with one clear action, not a list of five things to fix.

---

## Bell Work Answer Key (8 min)

**Prompt:** Before the practice assessment starts, write one Unit 4 strand you feel strongest in and one strand you most need to review tonight.

**Strong answer:** "I feel strongest in pedigree interpretation because I can justify the inheritance pattern with evidence. I most need to review biotechnology data because I still sometimes forget to mention the negative control."

**Grading:** Completion only. Use this as a conference-launch tool, not a score that adds pressure.

---

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

### 1. Bell Work and Launch (8 min)

Students complete the short self-assessment while you confirm materials and accommodations.

Transition cue:
> "Put your self-check face down on the corner of your desk and keep only the assessment packet in front of you."

### 2. Practice Assessment Directions (7 min)

Clarify expectations, timing, and what students should do if they finish early.

Say:
> "Today is diagnostic, not punitive. Show what you know honestly so tonight's review can be focused and useful."

### 3. Guided Practice Block 1 – Practice Assessment (30 min)

Students complete the first portion independently under assessment conditions.

### 4. Guided Practice Block 2 – Conferences and Corrections (30 min)

While students finish or begin corrections, pull short one-on-one conferences.

Conference structure:
- one strength
- one missed pattern
- one exact study target for tonight

### 5. Debrief and Exit Ticket (10 min)

Students record the one mistake they most want to avoid tomorrow and the one resource they will use tonight.

---

## Conference Prompts

- "What part of this response shows your strongest reasoning?"
- "Which missed item is most fixable before tomorrow?"
- "What exact page or practice set should you review tonight?"
- "What is the one mistake you do not want to repeat on the final?"

---

## Readiness Indicators

- Student explanations use specific vocabulary without prompting.
- Student work shows correct setup on major problem types.
- Student can identify one final review target accurately.

---

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

**Prompt:** What is the one mistake you most want to avoid tomorrow, and what will you review tonight to prevent it?

**Strong answer:** "I do not want to forget control comparisons on biotechnology questions. Tonight I will review my Day 9 notes and the guided-practice worksheet on gel interpretation."

**Grading:** 5 pts. Full credit for one specific mistake and one concrete review target.

---

## Final Support Moves

- Preserve assessment accommodations that will apply tomorrow.
- Keep corrections narrow and actionable.
- End with a short confidence reset so students leave focused, not overloaded.

---

## Differentiation

- **Advanced:** Let strong students complete one optional synthesis item after their conference if it keeps them calm and focused.
- **Support:** Highlight only the two most important corrections so the review load stays realistic.
- **ELL support:** Offer sentence starters for conferences such as "My strongest area is ___ because ___" and "I still need to review ___ because ___."

---

## Planning Note

Carry the conference sheet into Day 17. It becomes your best reminder of who needs a quiet reassurance, who needs accommodations checked twice, and who is most likely to rush preventable errors.

---

> "Lord, steady our students as they prepare for tomorrow. Replace panic with clarity, fear with trust, and confusion with honest focus. Amen."
