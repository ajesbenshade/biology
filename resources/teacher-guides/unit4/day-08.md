# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 8 – Complex Inheritance Patterns

## Scripture Focus

**Genesis 1:27**

Use the lesson to reinforce that human variation should be studied with both rigor and dignity.

## Teacher Objectives

- Teach incomplete dominance, codominance, and multiple alleles.
- Use ABO blood group as the anchor case.
- Help students choose the right inheritance model from evidence.

## Materials & Preparation

- Blood-type inheritance cards or worksheet
- Complex inheritance comparison set
- Model-selection chart

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Use triads when possible.
- Require an evidence statement before students label a pattern.
- Pull a short reteach group for codominance versus incomplete dominance confusion.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Strong answers correctly distinguish genotype from phenotype in a non-Mendelian example.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Genotype-phenotype warm-up.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model the three major non-Mendelian patterns.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Blood-type inheritance simulation.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Correct one flawed response and compare model fits.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Write one “watch out” note for complex inheritance.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong responses identify one reliable clue for the chosen inheritance model.

## Planning Note

Use today's work to identify who still needs explicit model-selection support.

## Prayer Close

Thank God for the diversity and order found in living systems.
