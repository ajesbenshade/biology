# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 13 – Biotechnology Tools and Applications

## Scripture Focus

**Micah 6:8**

Frame new scientific power with humility, justice, and wise use.

## Teacher Objectives

- Launch Chapter 13 with PCR, gel electrophoresis, recombinant DNA, and CRISPR basics.
- Keep discussion evidence-driven rather than purely opinion-based.
- Prepare students for data work on Day 14.

## Materials & Preparation

- Biotechnology scenario cards
- Simple biotechnology workflow diagram
- Tool-and-application comparison notes

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Groups must identify the relevant tool and expected evidence before debate or opinion.
- Use fixed group roles to keep discussion focused.
- Keep the first day of the chapter conceptual and structured.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Accept a plausible tool-to-problem match if students explain what kind of evidence would matter.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Match a biotechnology tool to the kind of problem it helps solve.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model PCR, gel electrophoresis, recombinant DNA, and CRISPR basics.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Analyze biotechnology scenarios for tool fit and evidence strength.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (27 min):** Label a biotechnology workflow and predict what evidence would show success.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Write one evidence-based claim about a biotechnology application.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong responses identify a tool clearly and name the kind of evidence needed to trust its result.

## Planning Note

Save strong tool-and-evidence examples to reuse during Days 14 and 15.

## Prayer Close

Ask for wisdom to use knowledge with humility and care.
