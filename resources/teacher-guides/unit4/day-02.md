# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 2 – DNA Structure and Replication

## Scripture Focus

**1 Corinthians 14:33**

Use the ordered structure and faithful replication of DNA as a reminder that students are studying a creation marked by coherence rather than confusion. Tie scientific accuracy to stewardship: careful diagnosis and careful repair are practices of responsible dominion.

## Teacher Objectives

- Teach DNA nucleotide structure, complementary base pairing, and antiparallel strand direction.
- Model semi-conservative replication clearly with helicase and DNA polymerase named accurately.
- Establish the class error-check routine for molecular work: check direction, check pairs, check backbone.
- Train students to diagnose one precise structural error and justify the repair with evidence.

## Materials & Preparation

- K'nex DNA, Replication, and Transcription kit
- Replication flowchart
- Error Menu cards or projected list
- One teacher demonstration model
- Enough pieces for each group to build one correct model and one flawed model

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Use the cue: check direction, check pairs, check backbone.
- Keep each group on the same K'nex set for the full block so the hidden error is tracked accurately.
- Make sure students keep K'nex pieces sorted by original strand and new strand before they begin the replication step.
- Enforce the one-error rule so the diagnostic exchange measures reasoning instead of luck.
- Pull a short reteach group for strand-direction confusion before the exchange round begins.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Students should sketch a double-stranded DNA segment with correct complementary base pairing, a labeled sugar-phosphate backbone, and opposite strand direction.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** DNA sketch, label the backbone, and identify correct base pairs.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Review nucleotide structure, antiparallel strands, complementary pairing, and semi-conservative replication using the K'nex DNA, Replication, and Transcription kit.
3. **Creator's Code: Build, Flaw, and Restore (30 min):** Groups of 3 to 4 build one correct DNA section and one 12 to 18 base-pair flawed DNA section with exactly one hidden error from the Error Menu.
4. **Diagnostic Exchange and Repair (22 min):** Groups exchange flawed models only, diagnose the hidden issue using the routine, justify the fix aloud, and repair the model.
5. **Replication Demonstration and Debrief (10 min):** Invite groups to demonstrate semi-conservative replication on the repaired model and report which step protected accuracy best.

## Error Menu Examples

- mismatched base pair such as A-C or G-T
- one strand built in the wrong direction
- missing or mislinked sugar-phosphate backbone connection
- extra nucleotide inserted where it does not belong
- complementary strand built with one non-matching base during replication

## Diagnostic Look-Fors

- Students can point to the backbone, bases, and strand direction without guessing.
- Students explain why A-T and C-G are the expected pairs rather than naming them from memory only.
- Students identify semi-conservative replication as one old strand plus one new strand in each daughter DNA molecule.
- Students can name helicase and DNA polymerase in the right sequence and role.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong answers name a step such as correct strand direction, complementary pairing, or polymerase adding the proper nucleotide and explain why that step protects accurate copying.

## Planning Note

Save the most common K'nex modeling and strand-direction errors for the molecular workshop on Day 5, and preview that questions of fidelity and human intervention return later when students discuss biotechnology ethics and CRISPR stewardship.
