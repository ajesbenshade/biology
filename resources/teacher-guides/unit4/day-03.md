# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 3 – Gene Expression and Protein Synthesis

## Scripture Focus

**Romans 1:20**

Highlight the coherence students see when DNA, RNA, and protein fit together into one understandable system.

## Teacher Objectives

- Model transcription and translation accurately.
- Teach codon-table use as a reasoning skill rather than memorization.
- Prepare students for mutation analysis on Day 4.

## Materials & Preparation

- Codon tables
- Flowchart handout
- Short mRNA sequence set

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Students underline the least-confident step before partner work.
- Partner A decodes first and Partner B checks orientation, then roles switch.
- Pull a short stop-codon reteach group if needed.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Strong responses name DNA as the source, RNA as the message, and proteins as the product.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Explain how DNA information becomes a protein.
2. **Mini-Lesson (18 min):** Model the central dogma with one full example.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Complete transcription and translation practice.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (22 min):** Run a codon clinic and correct one planted error.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Share one decoding habit that improved accuracy.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong responses explain how the codon table connects a specific mRNA codon to an amino acid choice.

## Planning Note

Keep strong sequence examples visible for Day 4 mutation tracing.

## Prayer Close

Thank God for the coherence of living systems and for the wisdom to trace them carefully.
