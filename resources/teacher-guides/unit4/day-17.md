# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 17 – Unit Assessment and Wrap-Up

> **For teacher use only.** This guide contains grading guidance, reflection facilitation notes, and Unit 5 bridge notes not intended for student view.

---

## Scripture Focus

**Psalm 139:14**
> "I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made; your works are wonderful, I know that full well."

Close the unit where it began: with awe at the design of living things and gratitude for growth in understanding.

---

## Teacher Objectives

- Administer the Unit 4 summative assessment fairly and consistently.
- Facilitate a meaningful reflection after the distributed practice and final mastery sequence.
- Provide a clear bridge into Unit 5.

**Background for you:** Keep this day clean and calm. The assessment should measure genetics understanding, not confusion about procedures. Reflection matters, but it should stay short and purposeful after the testing block.

---

## Key Biblical Integration Points

- "Assessment is not the measure of a student's worth. It is one measure of current understanding."
- "Reflection after hard work is an act of gratitude because it helps students notice what God has grown in them."
- "Closing the unit with wonder keeps the science tied to worship rather than reducing it to mere performance."

---

## Materials & Preparation

- Unit 4 summative assessment copies and any alternate formats or accommodations.
- Seating chart or testing arrangement prepared before class.
- Short written reflection prompt for the final minutes.
- One brief Unit 5 preview slide or note for the closing transition.

---

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Set testing materials out before students enter so the launch is quiet and immediate.
- Read directions once clearly, then keep clarifications limited to procedure rather than content.
- Keep reflection materials separate from the test so students do not move early into the closing task.
- Use the same calm tone throughout the block; this is not the day for last-minute reteach speeches.

---

## Bell Work Answer Key (5 min)

**Prompt:** Before the assessment begins, write one reminder to yourself about how you will work carefully today.

**Strong answers:**
- "I will check controls before making any biotechnology claim."
- "I will label genotype and phenotype separately before writing the final ratio."
- "I will slow down on codon charts and read each base in the correct order."

**Grading:** Completion only. Use this to settle students and focus their attention.

---

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

### 1. Quiet Launch and Focus Reminder (5 min)

Students complete the short reminder note while you finalize attendance, seating, and materials.

Transition cue:
> "Set the reminder note aside, keep only your assessment materials out, and wait for the directions."

### 2. Assessment Directions (5 min)

Review timing, expectations, and what students should do if they finish early.

### 3. Assessment Block (65 min)

Administer the Unit 4 summative assessment.

Teacher moves:
- monitor pacing and accommodations
- answer procedural questions only
- note any visible stress or rushing patterns for later reflection

### 4. Reflection Prompt (5 min)

When students finish, collect assessments first and then pass out the reflection slip.

### 5. Wrap-Up and Unit 5 Bridge (5 min)

Close the unit with one brief reflection share or teacher summary, then preview the shift into Unit 5.

---

## Reflection Prompts

- What is one Unit 4 idea you can explain better now because of the distributed practice?
- Which kind of evidence or problem solving now feels more manageable?
- What habit from Unit 4 should carry into Unit 5?

---

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

**Prompt:** What is one Unit 4 idea you can explain better now because of the distributed practice?

**Strong answer:** "I can explain inheritance patterns better now because repeated clinic practice taught me to separate genotype from phenotype and justify the pattern with evidence instead of guessing."

**Grading:** Completion or 5 pts, depending on local policy. Look for one clear idea and one reason it improved.

---

## Differentiation

- **Advanced:** If time remains after testing, invite students to add one optional sentence about which Unit 4 connection most prepared them for Unit 5.
- **Support:** Keep the reflection prompt narrow and concrete so tired students can still respond clearly.
- **ELL support:** Offer sentence frames such as "I understand ___ better now because ___" and "One habit I will carry into Unit 5 is ___."

---

## Planning Note

When scoring, compare persistent error patterns to your Day 10-16 conference and checkpoint notes. That will tell you whether remaining gaps are conceptual, procedural, or simply due to test pressure.
