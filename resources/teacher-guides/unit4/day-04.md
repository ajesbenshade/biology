# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 4 – Mutations and Protein Variation

## Scripture Focus

**Proverbs 18:13**

Frame the lesson as careful evidence tracing: students should examine the whole sequence before claiming an effect.

## Teacher Objectives

- Distinguish substitution, insertion, and deletion mutations.
- Trace a sequence change through codon and protein outcomes.
- Keep students focused on evidence rather than broad speculation.

## Materials & Preparation

- Mutation comparison sheet
- Sequence examples for whole-class modeling
- One frameshift sample to analyze together

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Students highlight the exact mutation location first.
- Partner checks begin with the first affected codon.
- Pause once to model how a frameshift changes downstream reading.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Students should identify the changed base correctly before naming the mutation type.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Compare two short sequences.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model substitution versus frameshift effects.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Classify mutation types from DNA-to-protein examples.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (27 min):** Complete a mutation clinic and short written explanation.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Record one reliable clue for identifying frameshifts.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong responses identify the mutation type and cite the sequence clue that made it clear.

## Planning Note

Use today's most common errors to shape the molecular workshop on Day 5.

## Prayer Close

Thank God for the precision of biological information and for the discipline to examine evidence fully.
