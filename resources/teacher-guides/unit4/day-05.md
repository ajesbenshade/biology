# Teacher Guide: Unit 4 Day 5 – Molecular Genetics Problem Solving

## Scripture Focus

**Colossians 3:23**

Use the workshop day to frame careful revision as worthwhile work, not wasted time.

## Teacher Objectives

- Strengthen molecular problem setup and explanation.
- Help students find the first place a sequence-based answer goes wrong.
- Gather evidence for Day 6 checkpoint support.

## Materials & Preparation

- Mixed molecular practice set
- Error-analysis samples
- Short conference tracker

## Routine and Logistics Notes

- Students must mark the first error line before revising.
- Keep station timing visible.
- Teacher conferences should end with one immediate retry.

## Bell Work Answer Key

Students should identify the first incorrect step in the flawed response, not just the final wrong answer.

## Lesson Delivery Script (85 min)

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Diagnose a flawed molecular response.
2. **Mini-Lesson (12 min):** Model one full DNA-to-protein problem.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (30 min):** Rotate through short molecular problem sets.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Run partner error checks and teacher conferences.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Name one process move that improved performance.

## Exit Ticket Answer Key

Strong answers point to one specific habit such as checking the first codon shift or confirming strand direction before solving.

## Planning Note

Group students for Day 6 based on which molecular strand still causes the most errors.

## Prayer Close

Ask for patience, discipline, and honesty in revision.
