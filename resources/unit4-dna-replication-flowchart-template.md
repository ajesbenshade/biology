# Unit 4 DNA Replication Flowchart Template

Prepared for Dock Mennonite High School Freshman Biology to help students trace DNA replication with scientific precision and orderly reasoning.

## Purpose

Use this template for Unit 4 Day 2 homework or guided practice. Students should complete each step with vocabulary, arrows, and short annotations that explain what is happening and why replication remains accurate.

## Flowchart Sequence

1. **Start with a double-stranded DNA molecule.**
   Label the sugar-phosphate backbone, complementary base pairs, and strand direction.
2. **Helicase unzips the DNA.**
   Show the two strands separating so the bases are exposed.
3. **Each original strand serves as a template.**
   Mark the original strands clearly so students can identify what remains from the first molecule.
4. **DNA polymerase adds complementary nucleotides.**
   Add A-T and C-G pairs correctly while preserving strand direction.
5. **Two complete DNA molecules form.**
   Show that each new DNA molecule contains one original strand and one newly built strand.
6. **State the conclusion.**
   Write one sentence explaining why this process is called semi-conservative replication.

## Required Accuracy Checks

- Label strand direction correctly.
- Use only complementary pairs A-T and C-G.
- Keep the sugar-phosphate backbone intact.
- Identify helicase and DNA polymerase by name.
- Mark one original strand and one new strand in each finished DNA molecule.

## Reflection Prompt

- Which step in your flowchart mattered most for replication accuracy, and why?
