# Unit 4 Study Guide

Use this guide to review the major ideas, vocabulary, and skills from Unit 4. Start with the big ideas, then practice diagrams, data questions, and short written explanations.

## Big Ideas to Master

### Chapter 11 — DNA, RNA, and Proteins
- DNA stores genetic information in a double-helix structure.
- DNA replication copies genetic material before cell division.
- Genes are expressed through transcription and translation.
- Mutations can change DNA sequences and affect proteins.

### Chapter 12 — Genetic Variation and Heredity
- Meiosis produces gametes and increases variation.
- Inheritance patterns can be modeled with Punnett squares and probability.
- Pedigrees help track inherited traits through generations.
- Chi-square analysis compares expected and observed outcomes.

### Chapter 13 — Biotechnology
- PCR amplifies DNA.
- Gel electrophoresis separates DNA fragments by size.
- Genetic engineering changes DNA intentionally.
- Biotechnology raises both scientific and ethical questions.

## Skills Checklist

Make sure you can:

- label a DNA strand and identify complementary base pairing
- explain the roles of helicase, DNA polymerase, mRNA, tRNA, and ribosomes
- use a codon chart to determine an amino acid sequence
- solve monohybrid and simple dihybrid inheritance problems
- interpret a pedigree for dominant or recessive inheritance
- explain what a chi-square result means in plain language
- describe how PCR and gels are used in biotechnology
- discuss one ethical issue in genetic engineering using evidence and reasoning

## Quick Self-Check Questions
1. Why is complementary base pairing essential for DNA replication?
2. What is the difference between transcription and translation?
3. How does meiosis increase genetic variation?
4. What is the difference between genotype and phenotype?
5. Why is chi-square useful in genetics?
6. How does gel electrophoresis help scientists compare DNA samples?

## Practice Prompts
1. Draw and label a DNA double helix, including base pairs and sugar-phosphate backbone.
2. Explain the full path from DNA to protein using the terms transcription, mRNA, codon, translation, and ribosome.
3. Solve a Punnett square for a monohybrid cross and explain the expected genotype and phenotype ratios.
4. Study a simple pedigree and determine whether the trait is more consistent with dominant or recessive inheritance.
5. Explain one real-world use of PCR or gel electrophoresis.
6. Discuss one ethical issue in genetic engineering and explain why careful scientific reasoning matters.
