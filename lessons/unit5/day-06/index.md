# Day 6: Explorer Case Studies — Zebra Stripes and Green-Blooded Skinks

## Objectives

- Analyze hypothesis testing in real field biology case studies.
- Evaluate competing explanations for zebra stripe function.
- Explain convergent-evolution questions raised by green-blooded skinks.

## Key Terms

- hypothesis
- convergent evolution
- thermoregulation
- selective pressure
- bilirubin

## Textbook Content

- NG Explorer Brenda Larison’s work examines zebra-striping function, including fly-repulsion and thermoregulation hypotheses.
- Striped-coat horse comparisons provide testable predictions about biting-fly behavior.
- NG Explorer Christopher C. Austin investigates New Guinea green-blooded skinks and unusually high bilirubin levels.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Hypothesis Matrix (20 min):** Complete a matrix of claim, test method, and evidence quality for zebra stripe hypotheses.
2. **Data Walk (20 min):** Review summarized field findings from horse-coat fly interaction experiments.
3. **Skink Convergence Discussion (20 min):** Evaluate whether repeated traits imply convergent pathways, design constraints, or both.

**Sample answers:**
- Evidence can support one function more strongly without proving exclusivity.
- Convergent patterns can arise under similar environmental constraints and do not eliminate alternative interpretive frameworks.

## Critical Perspective

- Which case-study claims are most directly supported by experimental or field measurements?
- What assumptions are made when moving from correlation to causation in animal traits?
- How can repeated traits be interpreted through both evolutionary and design-based lenses?
- Where should scientists be explicit about unresolved questions?

## Biblical Integration

Field biology demonstrates that creation is rich, complex, and worthy of careful observation. Christian students can value empirical testing while remaining open about interpretation limits and purpose.

## Homework / Assignment

- Write a one-page comparison of the zebra and skink case studies using evidence tables.
- Complete five short-response questions on hypothesis quality.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

Which case study better demonstrates strong experimental design, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Require students to justify conclusions with specific case-study observations.
- Keep tone respectful when discussing multiple interpretations.
