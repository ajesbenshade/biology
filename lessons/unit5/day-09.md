# Day 9: Genetic Variation, Gene Pools, and Allele Frequency

## Objectives

- Define gene pool and allele frequency in population genetics.
- Use Cepaea nemoralis snail data to calculate frequency changes.
- Explain how variation provides raw material for selection.

## Key Terms

- gene pool
- allele frequency
- phenotype frequency
- variation
- microevolution

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 15.2 introduces population-level genetics as the basis for microevolution.
- Snail populations (Cepaea nemoralis) provide visible trait-frequency examples.
- Allele-frequency shifts can be quantified and compared over time.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Snail Data Table (25 min):** Calculate phenotype frequencies from class dataset.
2. **Allele Estimation (20 min):** Estimate allele frequencies under simplified assumptions.
3. **Trend Interpretation (15 min):** Identify possible drivers of frequency change.

**Sample answers:**
- A gene pool is the set of all alleles in a population.
- If dark-shell phenotype frequency increases from 0.32 to 0.47, that indicates measurable population change.

## Critical Perspective

- Which calculations are direct and objective in population genetics?
- When moving from frequency change to causal explanation, what assumptions are added?
- How does observed microevolution differ from broader macroevolutionary claims?
- Why should students avoid overstating what one population dataset can prove?
