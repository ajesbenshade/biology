# Day 13: Sexual Selection and Behavioral Evidence

## Objectives

- Define sexual selection and explain mate-choice and competition models.
- Analyze lekking behavior data (including sage grouse displays).
- Distinguish reproductive-success evidence from survival-selection evidence.

## Key Terms

- sexual selection
- lek
- mate choice
- competition
- reproductive success

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 15.3 introduces sexual selection as a mechanism affecting mating success.
- Lekking systems (e.g., sage grouse) provide observable behavioral patterns and measurable outcomes.
- Trait persistence can be linked to differential reproductive success rather than direct survival advantage.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Behavior Graph Analysis (20 min):** Interpret lek display frequency vs. mating success.
2. **Video Observation Log (20 min):** Record behaviors and classify evidence type.
3. **Argument Workshop (20 min):** Build a CER explanation for one trait pattern.

**Sample answers:**
- Sexual selection can favor traits that increase mating opportunity.
- A trait may persist if reproductive advantage outweighs energetic cost.
- Behavioral data should be interpreted carefully because context and confounders matter.

## Critical Perspective

- Which aspects of sexual-selection claims are directly measured in behavioral datasets?
- What assumptions enter when explaining why a display trait originated historically?
- How can purposeful design and ecological pressures both be considered in interpretation?
- What would stronger evidence look like for competing explanations?

## Biblical Integration
