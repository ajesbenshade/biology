# Day 12: Gene Flow and Genetic Drift

## Objectives

- Define gene flow and genetic drift and compare their effects on allele frequencies.
- Explain bottleneck and founder effects with population examples.
- Model random drift outcomes across repeated trials.

## Key Terms

- gene flow
- genetic drift
- bottleneck effect
- founder effect
- random sampling

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 15.3 presents gene flow as allele movement among populations.
- Genetic drift describes random frequency shifts, especially in small populations.
- Bottleneck and founder events can cause rapid genetic composition changes.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Migration Scenario Analysis (15 min):** Predict gene-flow impact in connected populations.
2. **Drift Simulation (30 min):** Repeated coin/allele-draw trials compare small vs. large populations.
3. **Case Comparison (20 min):** Classify sample cases as bottleneck, founder effect, or gene flow.

**Sample answers:**
- Gene flow tends to increase similarity between populations.
- Drift effects are stronger in smaller populations due to sampling variation.
- A few individuals colonizing a new island is a founder-effect scenario.

## Critical Perspective

- Which mechanism in today’s simulations is random vs. directional?
- How do sample size and repetition influence confidence in conclusions?
- What claims can be made from drift data without overgeneralizing?
- Why is it important to separate observed mechanism from broader origin narratives?
