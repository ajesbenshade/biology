# Day 7: Chapter 14 Review and Evidence Evaluation

## Objectives

- Classify review questions by evidence type and claim strength.
- Distinguish homologous, analogous, and vestigial examples accurately.
- Explain when carbon-14 dating is and is not appropriate.

## Key Terms

- review synthesis
- homologous
- analogous
- vestigial
- carbon-14 limitation

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 14 review emphasizes micro/macro distinctions, fossil evidence, structure comparisons, and radiometric limits.
- Students integrate case studies (sloths, zebra stripes, skinks) into evidence-quality evaluation.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Review Stations (35 min):** Five stations: fossils, structures, embryology, molecular data, isotope dating.
2. **Error Analysis (20 min):** Correct flawed sample answers from a mock chapter review.
3. **Quick Check (10 min):** 12-item mixed formative quiz.
4. **Quiz Readiness (optional):** Short Chapter 14 checkpoint for targeted reteach grouping.

**Sample answers:**
- Carbon-14 is best for relatively recent once-living material, not very old igneous rocks.
- Analogous structures share function but not necessarily structural origin.

## Critical Perspective

- Which Chapter 14 evidence line has the strongest direct observational support?
- Where do chapter-review answers risk overstating certainty?
- How can students present multiple interpretations while still making evidence-based judgments?
- What does rigorous science communication look like when uncertainty exists?

## Biblical Integration

Truthfulness in science includes careful wording, honest uncertainty, and fair treatment of evidence. These habits align with Christian integrity and stewardship of knowledge.
