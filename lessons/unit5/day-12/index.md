## Objectives
- Summarize key findings from Christopher C. Austin’s green-blooded skink research.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Explorer read-through (15 min):** Annotate major findings and unresolved questions.
2. **Hypothesis matrix (20 min):** Compare possible explanations and required evidence.
3. **Evidence-quality discussion (20 min):** Identify strongest and weakest claim support.
4. **Short synthesis write (10 min):** Explain one supported claim and one open question.

**Sample answers (placeholder):**
- High bilirubin appears repeatedly and may confer selective advantages, but mechanism confirmation needs additional data.
- Repeated traits can be interpreted with multiple models; evidence strength matters more than assertion confidence.

## Critical Perspective
- Which claims in the skink study are strongly supported by direct observations?
- Where do convergent-evolution explanations rely on inference beyond observed physiology?
- How can common-design and common-ancestry frameworks both engage the same data honestly?
- What makes a cautious scientific conclusion more trustworthy?

## Biblical Integration
Creation studies can spark wonder and careful reasoning together. Students can ask deep biological questions while affirming God’s wisdom and purpose in the living world.
