# Day 12: National Geographic Explorer — Christopher C. Austin (Green Blood in Skinks)

## Objectives
- Summarize major findings from Christopher C. Austin’s research on green-blooded skinks.
- Evaluate competing hypotheses for why high biliverdin levels might be advantageous.
- Distinguish what is strongly supported from what remains unresolved.
- Practice explaining open scientific questions without overstating certainty.

## Key Terms
- biliverdin
- convergent evolution
- hypothesis
- parasite pressure
- adaptation

## Textbook Content
- The explorer feature highlights green-blooded skinks in New Guinea as an unusual and still-developing research case.
- Students analyze what has been measured directly, what hypotheses are currently strongest, and what evidence is still needed.
- The lesson extends earlier discussion by focusing more closely on Austin’s methods and interpretation.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Explorer read-through (15 min):** Annotate key findings, direct measurements, and unresolved questions.
2. **Hypothesis matrix (20 min):** Compare two or more explanations for green blood and the evidence behind each.
3. **Evidence-quality discussion (20 min):** Identify the strongest current support and the most important missing test.
4. **Short synthesis write (10 min):** State one supported claim and one open question.

**Sample answers:**
- High biliverdin may provide protection against parasites, but that claim still needs stronger in vivo evidence.
- The case is a strong example of scientific caution: some conclusions are well supported, while others remain open.

## Critical Perspective
- Which claims in this case are based on direct measurements?
- Why is in vitro evidence useful but not identical to in vivo confirmation?
- How can the same data invite more than one interpretation while still favoring some hypotheses over others?
- Why does careful scientific caution increase trust rather than weaken it?

## Biblical Integration
The living world still surprises us, and that surprise can deepen wonder rather than diminish faith. Students can study unusual cases honestly while recognizing God’s wisdom in creation and valuing intellectual humility.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete the explorer-guided questions.
- Write a paragraph identifying one claim this case supports well and one claim that still needs more evidence.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
What single piece of evidence from today most changed your understanding of this case, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Keep students from collapsing “promising hypothesis” into “proven conclusion.”
- Encourage evidence-centered discussion rather than confidence-centered assertion.
