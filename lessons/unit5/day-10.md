# Day 10: Types of Selection and Population Shifts

## Objectives

- Distinguish stabilizing, directional, and disruptive selection.
- Interpret bell-curve changes under different selection pressures.
- Explain how selection changes population distributions over time.

## Key Terms

- stabilizing selection
- directional selection
- disruptive selection
- distribution
- selective pressure

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 15.2 presents three common patterns of selection and expected trait-distribution outcomes.
- Bell-curve visuals model how trait frequencies shift under environmental pressure.
- These population-level shifts are central to microevolutionary analysis.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Curve Matching (15 min):** Match scenario cards to selection type.
2. **Bean Population Lab (30 min):** Simulate changing selection pressure and graph outcomes.
3. **Graph Interpretation (20 min):** Compare class bell curves before and after selection.

**Sample answers:**
- Stabilizing selection narrows variation around the mean.
- Directional selection shifts the mean trait value.
- Disruptive selection increases both extremes and can reduce the middle phenotype.

## Critical Perspective

- Which selection conclusions are most strongly supported by your graph data?
- How can short-term modeled change be overextended into long-term historical claims?
- What additional evidence is needed before making broader origin conclusions?
- How does acknowledging model limits strengthen scientific credibility?
