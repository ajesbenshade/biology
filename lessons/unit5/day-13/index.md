# Day 13: Looking at the Data: Tracking Evolution (Deer Mice) + Chapter 15 Review

## Objectives
- Analyze deer mouse coat-color data and calculate allele frequencies.
- Use Hardy-Weinberg reasoning to compare expected and observed population patterns.
- Review Chapter 15 mechanisms and evidence types.
- Distinguish measured population change from broader interpretive claims.

## Key Terms
- Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium
- allele frequency
- genotype frequency
- phenotype distribution
- review synthesis

## Textbook Content
- The deer mice dataset gives students a quantitative way to track population change using coat-color data.
- Hardy-Weinberg provides a comparison model for expected genotype frequencies when no major forces are acting.
- Chapter 15 review pulls together natural selection, other mechanisms, and evidence-versus-inference thinking.

![Deer mice tracking](../../../images/unit5/deer-mice-tracking.jpg)
"Looking at the Data: deer mouse coat-color tracking and population analysis."

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Data-table analysis (25 min):** Calculate allele and genotype values from the deer mouse dataset.
2. **Hardy-Weinberg check (20 min):** Compare expected and observed frequencies and propose likely explanations for deviation.
3. **Review stations (20 min):** Revisit Chapter 15 mechanisms, evidence types, and vocabulary.
4. **Answer correction (10 min):** Revise one weak response using evidence and precise language.

**Sample answers:**
- If dark-fur frequency increases in a dark environment, the pattern is consistent with selection favoring camouflage.
- A departure from Hardy-Weinberg suggests that one or more equilibrium conditions were not met, but it does not automatically identify the cause.

## Critical Perspective
- What does the deer mouse dataset directly demonstrate about population change?
- Why does Hardy-Weinberg function as a null model rather than a description of all real populations?
- Which claims in a review response are strongest when tied to quantitative evidence?
- How does mathematical reasoning help students avoid speculation?

## Biblical Integration
Careful analysis of data is a form of truthful stewardship. Students can honor God by calculating accurately, interpreting cautiously, and distinguishing measurable patterns from broader assumptions.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete the Chapter 15 review set.
- Write one reflection comparing measured population change with a broader interpretive claim.
- Finish any unfinished Hardy-Weinberg calculations.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
Identify one Chapter 15 statement supported by strong direct evidence and one statement that depends heavily on broader assumptions.

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Prioritize mathematical accuracy and evidence use over rhetorical confidence.
- Remind students that deviation from equilibrium identifies a problem to explain, not a cause by itself.
