# Day 14: Chapter 15 Review and Unit 5 Synthesis

## Objectives

- Synthesize Chapter 15 mechanisms into a coherent population-change framework.
- Defend evidence-based claims using vocabulary and data from Unit 5.
- Reflect on how rigorous science and Christian faith can be integrated thoughtfully.

## Key Terms

- synthesis
- mechanism
- evidence evaluation
- model assumption
- stewardship

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 15 review integrates natural selection, variation, Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium, gene flow, genetic drift, and sexual selection.
- Unit synthesis requires comparing mechanism-level evidence with larger historical interpretations.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Mechanism Concept Map (20 min):** Build a visual map connecting all Chapter 15 terms.
2. **Mixed Review Stations (30 min):** Solve data-based practice prompts from Days 8–13.
3. **Synthesis Writing (20 min):** Compose a final CER response with one evidence set and one interpretation critique.

**Sample answers:**
- Hardy-Weinberg provides a baseline to detect when evolutionary mechanisms are acting.
- Gene flow and drift can shift allele frequencies without necessarily reflecting directional adaptation.
- Strong scientific writing distinguishes observations, model assumptions, and conclusions.

## Critical Perspective

- Which Unit 5 claims are strongest because they are repeatedly observed and measured?
- Where do broad historical narratives rely on assumptions that should be named explicitly?
- How can common-design interpretation and common-ancestry interpretation be compared fairly?
- What habits help students evaluate claims with both openness and discernment?

## Biblical Integration

Christians can engage difficult scientific topics with confidence, humility, and intellectual honesty. By practicing careful evidence evaluation, students honor God with both mind and character.
