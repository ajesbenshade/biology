# Day 16: 16.1 Speciation

## Objectives
- Compare allopatric and sympatric speciation pathways.
- Explain adaptive radiation and common patterns of evolutionary change.
- Use evidence to answer diversification questions from real or modeled populations.

## Key Terms
- speciation
- allopatric speciation
- sympatric speciation
- adaptive radiation
- diversification

## Textbook Content
- Section 16.1 presents mechanisms of speciation including geographic isolation and within-range divergence.
- Adaptive radiation describes diversification from a shared starting population into multiple ecological niches.
- Pattern analysis questions ask students to identify factors that increase diversification likelihood.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Concept compare (15 min):** Venn diagram for allopatric vs sympatric speciation.
2. **Adaptive-radiation card sort (20 min):** Match species changes to niche differences.
3. **Identify prompt workshop (20 min):** Students answer the diversification-factors question with evidence.
4. **Scenario analysis (15 min):** Classify speciation examples and justify with mechanism language.
5. **Case-study extension (optional):** Analyze Galápagos finch diversification evidence using isolation and niche data.

**Sample answers:**
- Allopatric speciation typically involves physical separation and reduced gene flow.
- Sympatric speciation can occur without geographic separation when reproductive isolation develops.

## Critical Perspective
- Which speciation conclusions are directly supported by observed population data?
- Where do speciation narratives rely on assumptions about unobserved historical transitions?
- How should students evaluate claims that extend from local diversification to universal ancestry?
- Can repeated ecological patterns reflect both adaptation processes and purposeful design in creation?

## Biblical Integration
Studying biodiversity can deepen appreciation for the richness and order of life. Faith and science work together when we analyze evidence honestly and acknowledge both discovery and mystery.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete 16.1 guided questions and vocabulary practice.
- Reflection prompt: What evidence most clearly distinguishes allopatric from sympatric speciation?

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
In one sentence, state one condition that makes diversification more likely and explain why.

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Prioritize evidence-based explanations over speculative claims.
- Score for vocabulary precision, model accuracy, and reasoning clarity.
