# Day 17: 16.2 Extinction

## Objectives
- Distinguish background extinction, mass extinction, and local extinction outcomes.
- Identify major causes of population decline and vulnerability factors.
- Explain threatened vs endangered classifications and protection strategies.

## Key Terms
- extinction
- endangered
- threatened
- vulnerability
- population decline

## Textbook Content
- Section 16.2 examines extinction patterns, causes of decline, and legal/ecological protection categories.
- Vulnerability increases when populations are small, specialized, fragmented, or slow-reproducing.
- Knowledge Rating Scale vocabulary strategy helps students self-assess term understanding before and after instruction.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Knowledge Rating Scale (10 min):** Students rate familiarity with 16.2 vocabulary.
2. **Cause-impact matrix (20 min):** Link environmental pressures to extinction risk.
3. **Case classification (20 min):** Categorize species scenarios as threatened/endangered and justify.
4. **Protection plan mini-design (15 min):** Propose one action for reducing decline risk.
5. **Research extension:** Begin a short endangered-species profile with one evidence-based protection strategy.

**Sample answers:**
- Endangered status indicates immediate high risk of extinction in all or significant ranges.
- Specialized habitat needs often increase vulnerability to rapid environmental disruption.

## Critical Perspective
- Which extinction-risk claims are strongly supported by measurable population evidence?
- Where might students overgeneralize from one decline case to broad historical conclusions?
- How do assumptions about time scale influence extinction explanations?
- How should Christian stewardship shape responses to species vulnerability?

## Biblical Integration
Extinction discussions remind us that stewardship includes protecting life systems entrusted to human care. Scientific understanding helps us act responsibly toward neighboring communities and ecosystems.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete Section 16.2 review questions.
- Reflection prompt: Choose one vulnerability factor and explain how prevention could reduce extinction risk.
- Complete endangered-species summary with citation of one conservation dataset.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
What is one practical protection strategy that can reduce extinction risk for a vulnerable species?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Emphasize distinction between observed causes and inferred projections.
- Keep assessment criteria aligned to data interpretation and scientific vocabulary.
