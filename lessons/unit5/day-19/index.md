# Day 19: 16.4 Reducing Human Impact on the Environment

## Objectives
- Explain sustainability strategies for managing resources and reducing ecosystem damage.
- Evaluate pollution-reduction and habitat-restoration approaches.
- Justify why biodiversity conservation supports long-term ecosystem resilience.

## Key Terms
- sustainability
- resource management
- biodiversity
- habitat restoration
- pollution reduction

## Textbook Content
- Section 16.4 focuses on practical ways to reduce human impact through conservation and resource planning.
- Ecosystem restoration and pollution prevention are paired with biodiversity-maintenance goals.
- Semantic Map strategy organizes problem-solution links for environmental decision-making.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Semantic map build (15 min):** Connect impact categories to specific reduction strategies.
2. **School-practice audit (20 min):** Identify one campus practice that increases or decreases environmental impact.
3. **Solution ranking (20 min):** Evaluate proposed interventions by feasibility and expected impact.
4. **Short proposal (10 min):** Draft one evidence-based school/community action step.
5. **Case extension:** Evaluate a wildlife-corridor scenario and justify expected biodiversity effects.

**Sample answers:**
- Biodiversity maintenance improves ecosystem stability and adaptive capacity under change.
- Reducing pollution at source is often more effective than post-impact cleanup alone.

## Critical Perspective
- Which sustainability claims are supported by measurable outcomes, and which remain aspirational?
- How can students distinguish evidence-based stewardship from symbolic but low-impact actions?
- What assumptions underlie proposals about long-term environmental recovery?
- How can Christian care for creation motivate practical, evidence-aligned action?

## Biblical Integration
Stewardship is active, not passive: responsible care includes prevention, restoration, and wise resource choices. Science helps us evaluate which actions actually produce faithful outcomes.

Verses: Psalm 24:1; Genesis 2:15.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete 16.4 review questions.
- Reflection prompt: What is one realistic sustainability action your school could implement this semester?

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
Name one high-impact practice for reducing human environmental impact and defend it in one sentence.

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Guide students to evaluate feasibility and evidence, not just preferences.
- Maintain objective scoring tied to data interpretation and reasoning.
