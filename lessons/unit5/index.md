# Unit 5 — Evolution

In this unit we rigorously examine the evidence and mechanisms of evolutionary change as presented in National Geographic Biology (Chapters 14–16). Students master standard scientific models, data analysis, and case studies while applying critical scientific thinking to evaluate assumptions and interpretations. We view biological systems through faith in God as Creator (Colossians 1:16-17), asking how scientific observations help us better understand and appreciate His creation.

**Master these skills and you'll be ready for AP Biology or college-level science — equipped with both knowledge and discernment!**

## What You’ll Master

- Distinguish microevolution (observable allele-frequency changes) from macroevolution claims
- Evaluate fossil, anatomical, embryological, molecular, and biogeographical evidence
- Sequence fossilization, use index fossils, and interpret radiometric dating limits
- Identify homologous, analogous, and vestigial structures and apply cladistics
- Analyze natural selection plus mutation, gene flow, genetic drift, and sexual selection
- Use Hardy-Weinberg models to test population equilibrium assumptions
- Interpret Chapter 14–15 case studies using evidence-based reasoning
- Critically evaluate assumptions in evolutionary interpretations and common-design explanations
- Explain speciation mechanisms (allopatric and sympatric) and adaptive-radiation patterns
- Analyze extinction types, causes of population decline, and conservation protections
- Evaluate human environmental impacts and propose sustainability-focused solutions
- Model real-world scenarios such as tuskless-elephant trait shifts and ecological footprints

## Assessment Weighting

- **60% Summative** (chapter tests, unit exam, Tying-It-All-Together project)
- **40% Formative** (daily homework, bellwork, minilabs, discussions, reflections)
