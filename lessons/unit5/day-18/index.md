# Day 18: 16.3 Human Impact on the Environment + Minilab: Modeling Human-Caused Changes in the Environment

## Objectives
- Analyze how population growth, resource demand, and land-use decisions affect ecosystems.
- Explain impacts of water/air pollution, habitat loss, and land degradation.
- Model human-caused environmental change using a beetle-population simulation.

## Key Terms
- human impact
- habitat loss
- pollution
- land degradation
- resource use

## Textbook Content
- Section 16.3 connects human decisions to shifts in ecosystem stability and biodiversity.
- Environmental pressures include pollution, fragmentation, and altered resource availability.
- Minilab models dam construction effects on beetle populations using beads or paper punch-outs.

![Human impact minilab](../../../images/unit5/human-impact-minilab.jpg)
"Modeling human-caused changes: beetle population simulation setup."

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs
1. **Bellwork/Identify prompt (10 min):** List daily human decisions that affect local ecosystems and predict one climate-linked impact.
2. **Minilab setup (10 min):** Assign materials and define starting beetle-population conditions.
3. **Procedure rounds (25 min):** Simulate habitat changes and record population shifts.
4. **Results/analysis (20 min):** Graph outcomes and explain which pressures changed survival patterns.

**Sample answers:**
- Habitat fragmentation can quickly change trait frequency and reduce overall population size.
- Small populations showed greater instability after abrupt environmental modification.

## Critical Perspective
- What outcomes in the minilab are direct model results versus inferred real-world predictions?
- How can students avoid overextending a classroom model to every ecosystem context?
- Which human-impact claims are evidence-rich, and which need more data?
- How does stewardship responsibility shape scientific and policy decisions about resource use?

## Biblical Integration
Human choices influence creation systems, so stewardship must include informed action. Science provides tools to measure impact and guide restoration-oriented decisions.

Verses: Genesis 2:15; Micah 6:8.

## Homework / Assignment
- Complete minilab analysis questions and graph interpretation.
- Reflection prompt: Identify one local practice that could reduce human-caused ecosystem harm.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection
Which one human-caused factor in today’s model produced the largest population change, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)
- Reinforce careful interpretation of anthropogenic impact datasets.
- Grade responses for claim-evidence alignment and reasoning quality.
