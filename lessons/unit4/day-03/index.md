# Day 3: Gene Expression and Protein Synthesis

## Objectives

- Describe transcription from DNA to mRNA.
- Describe translation from mRNA to protein using a codon table.
- Decode short sequences and explain how biological information becomes a protein.

## Key Terms

- transcription
- translation
- mRNA
- codon
- ribosome

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 11 coverage of the central dogma.
- Codon-based translation practice and protein outcome interpretation.
- Sequence accuracy as the foundation for later mutation analysis.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Explain how a cell uses DNA information to build proteins.
2. **Mini-Lesson (18 min):** Model DNA to RNA to protein with sequence examples.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Complete transcription and translation flowchart practice.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (22 min):** Run a codon clinic in pairs and correct a planted decoding mistake.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Share one decoding habit that improved your accuracy.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students underline the step they feel least confident about before partner work begins.
- In the codon clinic, Partner A decodes while Partner B checks codon-table orientation, then roles switch.
- The teacher pulls a short support group if start and stop codons are being skipped.

## Critical Thinking

- How could one mistake in reading a sequence change a real scientific or medical conclusion about a protein?
- Why is it important to trace DNA to RNA to protein step by step when explaining what happened in a real cell?
