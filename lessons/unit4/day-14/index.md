# Day 14: Biotechnology Data Analysis

## Objectives

- Interpret PCR output and gel electrophoresis band patterns accurately.
- Use controls to evaluate whether a biotechnology result is trustworthy.
- Support a biotechnology conclusion with specific data.

## Key Terms

- PCR
- gel electrophoresis
- positive control
- negative control
- band pattern

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 13 data interpretation skills.
- Controls, band size, and comparison logic.
- Evidence habits that prepare students for CER writing on Day 15.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Identify which control matters most in a short gel description.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model how to read a gel and separate evidence from conclusion.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Interpret two biotechnology datasets with structured prompts.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Compare one strong and one weak data explanation, then revise the weak version.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Record one specific data detail that makes a claim stronger.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Data packets stay closed until the class labels controls together.
- Partner A names the data point first and Partner B checks whether the explanation actually uses it.
- The teacher pulls a short support cluster if students keep ignoring the negative control.

## Critical Perspective

- Why does a band by itself not automatically prove success?
- How do controls protect scientists from careless conclusions?

## Biblical Integration

Truthful scientific communication requires saying no more and no less than the evidence supports.

## Homework / Assignment

- Complete the biotechnology data practice set.
- Correct one weak explanation by adding a specific control comparison.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What data detail mattered most in today's analysis, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Keep the block focused on reading data well before expanding into full CER writing.
- Save strong evidence sentences as models for Day 15.
