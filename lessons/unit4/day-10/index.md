# Day 10: Mixed Practice Workshop and Small-Group Support

## Objectives

- Apply codon-table decoding to protein synthesis tasks.
- Solve mixed Punnett square and chi-square problems.
- Label DNA replication structures and identify mutation types.
- Use station evidence and teacher check-ins to identify the most useful next practice target.

## Key Terms

- codon table
- chi-square
- leading strand
- lagging strand
- mutation type

## Textbook Content

- Unit 4 cumulative skill integration from Chapters 11–13.
- Emphasis on accuracy, process clarity, and error correction.
- Small-group support based on the distributed checkpoints from earlier in the unit.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Complete one mixed-review problem with justification and identify which skill bucket it belongs to.
2. **Mini-Lesson (12 min):** Frame the workshop, model one correction, and explain how students will use evidence from station work to choose next steps.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (30 min):** Complete station work on codon decoding, inheritance probability, replication labeling, and mutation classification.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Run partner error-checks, peer teaching, and short teacher pull-groups for students who need targeted support.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Solve one challenge synthesis problem and record the station that most needs more practice.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Station timing stays visible throughout the block, and students rotate only after submitting one written answer at the current station.
- During partner error-checks, one student marks the first mistake while the other explains the correction aloud before roles switch.
- The teacher pull-group runs at a side table during Guided Practice Block 2 while the rest of the class continues peer teaching independently.

## Critical Perspective

- Which mistakes came from calculation versus vocabulary misuse?
- How does careful process checking improve scientific reliability?
