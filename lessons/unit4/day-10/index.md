# Day 10: Inheritance Probability and Chi-Square

## Objectives

- Calculate inheritance probability from Punnett-square and pedigree evidence.
- Compare expected and observed ratios using chi-square reasoning.
- Explain what chi-square can and cannot tell us about a model.

## Key Terms

- probability
- expected ratio
- observed ratio
- chi-square
- chance variation

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 12 quantitative heredity skills.
- Chi-square as a tool for evaluating fit between observed and expected outcomes.
- Probability reasoning as an extension of earlier inheritance models.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Solve a short inheritance probability question and show your setup.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model expected versus observed ratios and walk through the chi-square formula.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Solve probability and chi-square problems with teacher checkpoints.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Compare one strong and one flawed chi-square interpretation and revise the weak explanation.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Record one habit that makes quantitative heredity work more reliable.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students must finish the setup line before they use calculators or arithmetic shortcuts.
- Partner checks focus on whether each category is computed separately before summing.
- The teacher uses a side table for students whose arithmetic is strong but interpretation is weak.

## Critical Perspective

- Why is chi-square a reasoning tool rather than a proof machine?
- How does quantitative evidence strengthen inheritance claims?

## Biblical Integration

Quantitative reasoning is one more way to describe the orderliness of biological patterns faithfully.
