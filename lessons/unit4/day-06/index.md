# Day 6: Human Genetics, Pedigrees, and Evidence Writing

## Objectives

- Construct and interpret pedigree charts using standard symbols.
- Distinguish autosomal and sex-linked inheritance clues.
- Calculate inheritance probabilities from pedigree evidence.
- Write a short evidence-based explanation that defends an inheritance claim with pedigree clues.

## Key Terms

- pedigree
- carrier
- autosomal
- sex-linked
- probability

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 12 heredity applications with family-based inheritance data.
- Pedigree conventions for generational pattern analysis.
- Explanation practice focused on using multiple pedigree clues before naming a pattern.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Interpret one simple pedigree, identify likely carriers, and mark the clue that led you there.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Review pedigree notation, common errors, and the difference between autosomal and sex-linked evidence.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Diagnose inheritance patterns from sample family trees with teacher checkpoints after each generation.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Compute recurrence risk in pedigree scenarios and write one short evidence-based paragraph defending an inheritance claim.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Share one pedigree clue that changed your conclusion and one explanation habit to keep using.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Bellwork starts in partner pairs, then students shift into table groups with assigned jobs: lead reader, clue finder, and recorder.
- Groups must identify at least two pedigree clues before they ask the teacher to confirm an inheritance pattern.
- During Guided Practice Block 2, the teacher pulls a quick conference group for students who leap to sex-linked conclusions without checking every generation.

## Critical Perspective

- What pedigree conclusions are strongly supported by observed family data?
- Where do limited sample sizes affect confidence in inheritance claims?

## Biblical Integration

Genetics in families should be studied with care, compassion, and integrity. Scientific knowledge is most useful when paired with respect for human dignity.

## Homework / Assignment

- Complete the pedigree analysis worksheet.
- Revise one short explanation of an inheritance pattern using at least two pedigree clues.
- Add a brief research summary on one genetic disorder.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What additional data would strengthen your confidence in a pedigree conclusion, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Advanced extension: Add a mixed autosomal/sex-linked challenge pedigree.
- Support for struggling learners: Provide guided annotation prompts and a sentence frame for citing evidence from multiple generations.
