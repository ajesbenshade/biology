# Day 6: Human Genetics and Pedigrees

## Objectives

- Construct and interpret pedigree charts using standard symbols.
- Distinguish autosomal and sex-linked inheritance clues.
- Calculate inheritance probabilities from pedigree evidence.
- Discuss genetic disorders with scientific care and compassion.

## Key Terms

- pedigree
- carrier
- autosomal
- sex-linked
- probability

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 12 heredity applications with family-based inheritance data.
- Pedigree conventions for generational pattern analysis.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (10 min):** Interpret one simple pedigree and identify likely carriers.
2. **Symbol Review (10 min):** Pedigree notation and common errors.
3. **Group Analysis (25 min):** Diagnose inheritance patterns from sample family trees.
4. **Probability Practice (20 min):** Compute recurrence risk in pedigree scenarios.
5. **Debrief (5 min):** Evidence defense for one inheritance claim.

## Critical Perspective

- What pedigree conclusions are strongly supported by observed family data?
- Where do limited sample sizes affect confidence in inheritance claims?

## Biblical Integration

Genetics in families should be studied with care, compassion, and integrity. Scientific knowledge is most useful when paired with respect for human dignity.

What additional data would strengthen your confidence in a pedigree conclusion?
