# Day 6: Chapter 11 Review and Assessment

## Objectives

- Demonstrate Chapter 11 readiness across DNA structure, replication, gene expression, and mutation.
- Correct lingering molecular genetics misconceptions before the unit shifts to heredity.
- Use assessment evidence to identify the most important next step for Chapter 12.

## Key Terms

- checkpoint
- molecular genetics
- correction
- readiness
- assessment evidence

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 11 cumulative review and checkpoint.
- Short correction cycle that keeps the extra chapter time embedded before the unit moves on.
- Transition framing from molecular information to inheritance patterns.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Write the Chapter 11 idea you feel strongest about and one idea you still need to tighten.
2. **Mini-Lesson (12 min):** Review two high-frequency Chapter 11 errors with corrected models.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Complete a short Chapter 11 review set in pairs.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Take a brief checkpoint, then correct one missed item immediately.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Reflect on one chapter strength and one target to carry into inheritance work.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students complete the checkpoint independently before any partner correction begins.
- Immediate correction time focuses on one missed item, not a full reteach.
- The teacher uses results to sort support priorities for the Chapter 12 launch.

## Critical Thinking

- If a chapter checkpoint reveals one weak molecular idea, how should that change the way you prepare for real heredity problems next?
- Which molecular genetics ideas matter most when you try to explain how a real trait can be inherited?

## Biblical Integration

Wise learning includes honest assessment, correction, and steady growth.

## Homework / Assignment

- Finish any incomplete corrections from the Chapter 11 checkpoint.
- Preview the first heredity vocabulary for Day 7.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What Chapter 11 idea can you now explain clearly, and what still needs one more review?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Keep the chapter close practical and focused so Chapter 11 ends before the unit shifts into heredity.
- Use checkpoint patterns to organize the opening of the Chapter 12 block.
