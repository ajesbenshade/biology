# Day 1: Introduction to Genetics – The Wonder of Inherited Traits

## Objectives

- Describe genes, chromosomes, and DNA using accurate biological vocabulary.
- Explain inheritance as transmission of biological information across generations.
- Connect observations of biological complexity to careful scientific reasoning.

## Key Terms

- gene
- chromosome
- DNA
- inherited trait
- variation

## Textbook Content

- National Geographic Chapter 11 opener introducing heredity and trait transmission.
- Foundational concepts: DNA as information, genes as coded instructions, chromosomes as DNA organization structures.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (10 min):** List three family traits you share and one unique trait, then propose one possible explanation for variation.
2. **Phenomenon Discussion (15 min):** Analyze trait photos and classify observations versus interpretations.
3. **Direct Instruction (20 min):** Guided notes on DNA, genes, and chromosomes with quick checks for understanding.
4. **Collaborative Activity (20 min):** Teams build a concept map linking DNA → genes → proteins → traits.
5. **Debrief (5 min):** Share one strong evidence-based statement about inheritance.

## Critical Perspective

- Which parts of heredity are directly observed, and which are inferred from models?
- How does clear use of evidence improve scientific claims about genetic traits?

## Biblical Integration

God’s creation is orderly and intelligible, which supports careful scientific study. Human life is described as fearfully and wonderfully made, inviting both rigor and humility in genetics.

## Homework / Assignment

- Read Chapter 11.1.
- Complete the key-terms organizer and one paragraph explaining how DNA information relates to visible traits.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What is one genetics claim from class that is directly supported by observed evidence?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Advanced extension: Research one genetic disorder and distinguish observed data from interpretation.
- Support for struggling learners: Provide partially completed diagrams and sentence stems.
