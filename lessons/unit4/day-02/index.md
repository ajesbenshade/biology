# Day 2: DNA Structure and Replication

Today students study DNA as a highly ordered information system. The structure of each nucleotide, the faithful pairing of bases, and the semi-conservative pattern of replication all point to a creation that is intelligible rather than chaotic.

## Scripture Focus

**1 Corinthians 14:33** reminds students that God is not the author of confusion. The ordered, faithful replication of DNA provides a concrete example of biological design that rewards careful observation rather than careless guessing.

## Objectives

- Master DNA nucleotide structure by identifying sugar, phosphate, nitrogen base, and the way nucleotides connect into a strand.
- Explain why complementary base pairing follows the specific pattern of A-T and C-G.
- Model antiparallel DNA strands with correct 5' to 3' and 3' to 5' direction.
- Explain semi-conservative replication and the roles of helicase and DNA polymerase in accurate copying.
- Build diagnostic habits by applying the class error-check routine: check direction, check pairs, check backbone.

## Key Terms

- nucleotide
- sugar-phosphate backbone
- complementary base pairing
- antiparallel
- semi-conservative replication
- helicase
- DNA polymerase

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 11 coverage of DNA structure, replication, and the logic of molecular accuracy.
- DNA as a double-stranded molecule built from repeating nucleotides, a stable sugar-phosphate backbone, and complementary nitrogen bases.
- Semi-conservative replication as a process in which each daughter DNA molecule keeps one original strand and receives one newly built strand.
- Enzyme roles in strand separation and copying, with attention to helicase opening the double helix and DNA polymerase adding correct complementary nucleotides.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Sketch a short DNA segment from memory, label the sugar-phosphate backbone, and mark which bases pair together.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Use the K'nex DNA, Replication, and Transcription kit to model nucleotide structure, antiparallel strands, complementary base pairing, and the sequence of semi-conservative replication.
3. **Creator's Code: Build, Flaw, and Restore (30 min):** In groups of 3 to 4, students build two DNA sections that are 12 to 18 base pairs long. One model must be completely correct. The second model must contain exactly one secret error chosen from the Error Menu, such as a mismatched base pair, reversed strand direction, or incorrect backbone linkage.
4. **Diagnostic Exchange and Repair (22 min):** Groups exchange only the flawed model. The receiving group uses the routine check direction, check pairs, check backbone to diagnose the problem, explain why it is inaccurate, and repair the model with precise vocabulary.
5. **Replication Demonstration and Debrief (10 min):** Groups optionally demonstrate semi-conservative replication on the repaired model, then report which step most protected accuracy and which error was easiest to miss.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students always begin diagnosis by checking strand direction before touching any pieces.
- Groups keep K'nex pieces sorted by original strand and new strand so the replication model stays clear during the build.
- The class pauses at each checkpoint and repeats the cue aloud: check direction, check pairs, check backbone.
- The teacher pulls a short reteach table for students who confuse antiparallel strands or mix up template and newly added nucleotides.

## Critical Thinking

- If DNA copying errors build up in real cells, what consequences might show up in tissues or organisms over time?
- What evidence supports the claim that DNA replication is semi-conservative rather than fully conservative or completely random?

## Biblical Integration

The order and reliability of DNA replication reflect God's trustworthy design. Students are not studying a confusing accident but a coherent system that can be investigated carefully. Careful diagnosis and repair also model stewardship: when students identify an error truthfully and correct it responsibly, they practice the kind of disciplined attention that honors the Creator.

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Keep the focus on nucleotide structure, complementary pairing, and antiparallel direction before moving students into faster replication explanations.
- Suggested Error Menu items: mismatched bases, one strand reversed in direction, a broken or mislinked sugar-phosphate backbone, an extra nucleotide that does not belong, or a polymerase step that adds a non-complementary base.
- Require each flawed model to contain exactly one hidden error so diagnosis stays rigorous rather than turning into guessing.
- Use the phrase check direction, check pairs, check backbone every time groups pause; consistency matters more than speed on the first molecular modeling day.
- When you preview the biotechnology block, note that faithful copying matters again when students later discuss gene editing and CRISPR stewardship.
