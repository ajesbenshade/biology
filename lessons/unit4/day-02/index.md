# Day 2: DNA Structure, Replication, and Error Checking

## Objectives

- Model DNA structure using base-pairing rules.
- Explain semi-conservative replication with correct enzyme roles.
- Use an error-check routine to diagnose common replication mistakes before they become habits.

## Key Terms

- double helix
- complementary base pairing
- semi-conservative replication
- helicase
- DNA polymerase

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 11 DNA structure and replication overview.
- Enzyme sequence: strand separation, primer placement, nucleotide addition, and fragment joining.
- Replication checkpoint habits: verify strand direction, enzyme role, and complementary base pairing.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Sketch and label one DNA segment from memory, then check your partner's base-pairing accuracy.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Review DNA structure, strand direction, and the replication sequence with a teacher-modeled example.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Construct and label a DNA model with paired bases, strand direction, and a short written explanation of complementary pairing.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (27 min):** Complete a replication simulation, pause at two checkpoints to diagnose common student errors, and correct one flawed model.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Identify one replication error source, one correction mechanism, and one step you can now explain more clearly.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students complete the opening sketch independently, then exchange papers for a quick partner base-pairing check before the mini-lesson starts.
- In the model build, Partner A builds first while Partner B checks strand direction and labels; partners switch jobs halfway through the block.
- During the replication simulation, the teacher uses the cue "check direction, check pairs, check enzymes" and pulls a brief reteach table for students who reverse template and new strands.

## Critical Perspective

- Which replication steps are directly observed versus model-based representations?
- How do we evaluate claims about replication fidelity using evidence?
- How should mutation frequency be discussed without overstating conclusions?

## Biblical Integration

The reliability and order in biological information systems supports careful stewardship of scientific knowledge. Students practice truth-seeking through evidence, precision, and intellectual humility.

## Homework / Assignment

- Complete a replication flowchart.
- Correct one intentionally flawed replication diagram using precise enzyme vocabulary.
- Reflection prompt: What would happen if replication were not accurate?

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

Why does replication fidelity matter for inheritance across generations, and which step in the process protects that fidelity?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Advanced extension: Compare proofreading mechanisms across organisms.
- Support for struggling learners: Provide a labeled enzyme sequence reference and a partially completed error-check checklist.
