# Unit 4: Genetics

## Academic Excellence Focus (Core, Non-Negotiable)

- Mendelian ratios with chi-square analysis
- Meiosis/mitosis comparison with labeled diagrams
- DNA structure and replication (Watson-Crick model; leading/lagging strands; key enzymes)
- Transcription and translation with codon-table practice
- Gene regulation and mutations (point vs. frameshift)
- Biotechnology foundations (PCR, gel electrophoresis, CRISPR basics)
- Pedigree analysis and probability calculations

## Scripture Integration Approach

Each daily summary includes a boxed **Scripture Connection** with:
- A relevant verse
- A 2–3 sentence insight tied directly to measured scientific evidence
- One synthesis question requiring both data and biblical reflection

## Assessment Weighting

- **60% Objective:** tests, Punnett square mastery, data analysis, graphing
- **40% Application:** lab reports and claim-evaluation writing using data + Scripture

## Daily Lessons

### Chapter 11: DNA, RNA, and Proteins  
**Molecular Basis of Heredity**  
**Core Academic Goal:** Students will master the central dogma (replication → transcription → translation) with quantitative practice, data analysis, and modeling while recognizing these processes as the precise information system designed by our Creator (Colossians 1:16-17).  
**Pacing:** 8 days (college-prep pace with built-in review and labs)  
**Materials Used:** All provided Cengage resources (11.1–11.3 sections, Minilab, Looking at the Data, Case Study, Chapter Review, NG Explorer video).

**Day 1 – 11.1 Genetic Information: Discovery & Structure of DNA**  
**Objectives:** Discuss experiments that revealed DNA’s role; relate DNA, mRNA, and protein structures to their functions; analyze base-pairing rules.  
**Key Practice:** Complete base-pairing exercises and DNA vs. RNA comparison table (from 11.1).  
**Scripture Connection (insightful):**  
**Psalm 139:13-16** – “For you created my inmost being… all the days ordained for me were written in your book before one of them came to be.”  
**Tie-in:** The four-letter code (A-T, C-G) functions exactly like a book of instructions. Students calculate complementary strands and journal: “How does the precision of base pairing reflect the ‘book’ God wrote in every cell?”  
**Assessment:** Exit ticket – label a DNA model.

**Day 2 – 11.2 DNA Replication (Part 1)**  
**Objectives:** Relate DNA structure to its semi-conservative replication; identify roles of helicase, primase, DNA polymerase, and ligase.  
**Key Practice:** Diagram the replication fork (leading/lagging strands) and label enzymes (matches Chapter 11 Review question 3).  
**Scripture Connection:**  
**Colossians 1:17** – “He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together.”  
**Tie-in:** Proofreading enzymes achieve error rates lower than 1 in 10 billion. This is not chance—it is the sustaining power of Christ holding the genetic code together.  
**Assessment:** Enzyme matching quiz.

**Day 3 – 11.2 DNA Replication (Part 2) + Fidelity Analysis**  
**Objectives:** Explain outcomes of replication; calculate error rates using real data.  
**Key Practice:** Chi-square analysis of replication accuracy data.  
**Scripture Connection:** Same as Day 2 (reinforced).  
**Assessment:** Short constructed response comparing replication to a high-fidelity copier.

**Day 4 – 11.2 Transcription**  
**Objectives:** Describe how DNA information is copied into mRNA; explain RNA processing (introns/exons).  
**Key Practice:** Transcribe a DNA sequence to mRNA and identify start/stop signals.  
**Scripture Connection:**  
**John 1:1-3** – “In the beginning was the Word… Through him all things were made.”  
**Tie-in:** The genetic code is information transferred from DNA to mRNA. Students see that biological “words” (codons) originate from the eternal Word.

**Day 5 – 11.2 Translation & the Genetic Code**  
**Objectives:** Explain translation using codons; use a codon table to determine amino acids (matches Chapter 11 Review question 4 – CAU = histidine).  
**Key Practice:** Translate sample mRNA sequences; begin Minilab modeling.  
**Scripture Connection:** Same as Day 4 (reinforced).  
**Assessment:** Codon table worksheet + protein synthesis diagram.

**Day 6 – 11.3 Regulating Gene Expression + Epigenetics**  
**Objectives:** Compare prokaryotic and eukaryotic regulation; explain introns/exons and environmental effects on gene expression.  
**Key Practice:** Analyze “Looking at the Data: Decay of mRNA Molecules” graphs (E. coli vs. human half-lives).  
**Scripture Connection:**  
**Psalm 104:24** – “How many are your works, Lord! In wisdom you made them all.”  
**Tie-in:** Cells do not express every gene at once; regulation is a wise design feature. Students argue from evidence why longer mRNA half-lives in complex organisms reflect purposeful design, not accident.

**Day 7 – Minilab & Case Study Application**  
**Objectives:** Model replication, transcription, and translation (full Minilab); connect to butterfly wing patterns (Case Study: Expressing the Genetic Code).  
**Key Practice:** Group modeling activity + NG Explorer Carter Clinton video (genetic anthropology tie-in).  
**Scripture Connection:**  
**Job 10:8-12** – “Your hands shaped me and made me… Did you not clothe me with skin and flesh…?”  
**Tie-in:** Environmental factors (temperature/light) switch genes on/off without changing the DNA code—exactly as God designed for diversity within kinds (butterfly adaptive radiation).  
**Assessment:** Lab write-up with photo of student model.

**Day 8 – Chapter 11 Review & Synthesis**  
**Objectives:** Master all Review Key Concepts (identify, classify, label, calculate, explain, summarize, describe).  
**Key Practice:** Full Chapter 11 Review packet + epigenetics example from butterflies.  
**Scripture Connection (capstone):**  
**Colossians 1:16-17** (review of unit philosophy).  
**Tie-in:** Students write a one-paragraph synthesis: “How do the mechanisms of DNA, RNA, and proteins demonstrate that ‘in Him all things hold together’?”  
**Assessment:** Chapter test (60% objective, 40% biblical integration/application).
