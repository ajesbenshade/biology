# Day 7: Mendelian Inheritance

## Objectives

- Solve monohybrid Punnett square problems accurately.
- Distinguish genotype and phenotype in inheritance analysis.
- Use evidence from allele combinations to explain trait predictions.

## Key Terms

- allele
- dominant
- recessive
- genotype
- phenotype

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 12 introduction to Mendelian inheritance.
- Predictive modeling with Punnett squares.
- Strong setup habits that support the full heredity chapter.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Predict outcomes from a simple dominant-recessive cross.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Model genotype versus phenotype and the setup of a monohybrid cross.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (27 min):** Complete a set of Punnett square problems with teacher checkpoints.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Compare predicted genotype ratios to phenotype outcomes and correct one flawed sample.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Record one setup habit that prevents inheritance errors.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students solve the first Punnett square independently before partner talk begins.
- Partner A sets up parent alleles and Partner B checks the inside boxes before roles switch.
- The teacher keeps the language precise so genotype and phenotype are never treated as interchangeable.

## Critical Perspective

- Why is inheritance reasoning stronger when the setup is visible?
- What happens when students jump straight to trait labels without checking allele combinations?

## Biblical Integration

Inheritance patterns reveal order that can be studied carefully and described truthfully.

- Complete the monohybrid practice set.
- Write one paragraph comparing genotype predictions to phenotype outcomes.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What is the most important step in a Punnett square setup, and why?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Keep this day tightly focused on monohybrid structure before layering more complexity.
- Save common setup errors for Days 10 and 11.
