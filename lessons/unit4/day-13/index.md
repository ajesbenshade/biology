# Day 13: Data and Reasoning Studio

## Objectives

- Interpret biotechnology datasets with stronger precision and use of controls.
- Strengthen claim-evidence-reasoning responses using specific data references.
- Connect biotechnology data reading to earlier Unit 4 reasoning habits instead of treating it as a separate skill.

## Key Terms

- control sample
- evidence quality
- CER
- amplification
- band pattern

## Textbook Content

- Chapter 13 biotechnology analysis skills with renewed emphasis on PCR and gel electrophoresis interpretation.
- Cumulative writing practice connecting data to defensible scientific claims.
- Spiral review of vocabulary and explanation habits from earlier lessons.

## In-Class Activities / Minilabs

1. **Bellwork (8 min):** Correct one weak CER response by improving the evidence statement.
2. **Mini-Lesson (15 min):** Teacher walks through one PCR dataset and one gel interpretation example, naming the evidence moves students already know.
3. **Guided Practice Block 1 (25 min):** Students annotate controls, identify patterns, and answer scaffolded prompts across two datasets.
4. **Guided Practice Block 2 (25 min):** Students write one full CER response, trade papers for peer feedback, and revise one sentence for stronger reasoning.
5. **Debrief (10 min):** Share one sentence stem or evidence move that improved reasoning quality.

## Classroom Routine Notes

- Students keep data packets closed until the teacher has modeled the first control comparison with the whole class.
- In peer review, one student reads the evidence sentence aloud while the partner checks for a specific control result or band comparison before any reasoning is discussed.
- The teacher pulls a short reteach group during the writing block if students still collapse evidence and reasoning into the same sentence.

## Critical Perspective

- What makes one biotechnology conclusion justified while another is overstated?
- How do controls protect scientists from making careless claims?

## Biblical Integration

Truthful scientific communication requires honesty about what the data shows and restraint about what it does not show.

## Homework / Assignment

- Revise today's CER response using peer and teacher feedback.
- Complete one additional biotechnology data prompt from the review packet.

## Exit Ticket / Reflection

What is one specific kind of evidence you used today that made your conclusion stronger, and how did it support the claim?

## Teacher Notes Only (not for students)

- Require students to cite a band size, control result, or specific comparison instead of vague language.
- Pull a short reteach group if students still confuse evidence with reasoning.
